The Gods of Castalia

The Castalians worship six principal gods, plus a multitude of lesser deities that are associated with particular places or cults. The gods are thought to have shaped the world, and rule over various aspects of it. Offerings are made to the gods in elaborate public rituals in exchange for their favor—for a good harvest, for health, et cetera.

It is considered unwise to insult a god, or snub them by refusing to perform the rituals they demand. Even Neesha, feared and disliked for the monsters and darkness she commands, still has a temple in most major cities. What good would it do to upset her?

Corentin, God of the World Above

Ruler of the sky, father of Soleil and Neesha. He oversees all that happens under the sky, whether during the day or night, and fairly judges the deeds of all humanoids. Every soul that leaves the world must pass through his domain on its way to the afterlife. His symbol is a silver cloud over a white mountain peak.

Soleil, Goddess of Light and Passion

Also known as the Lady of the Radiant Dawn, or the Brilliant Lady, among other epithets. She is often depicted as a beautiful young woman with bronzed skin and impossibly long and luminous hair. She represents the light of day, driving away the darkness of night. She is known for her intensity of emotion, as much as she is joyous and loving, she is also fierce and resolute. She often quarrels with Neesha. Her symbol is a yellow sun with eight rays.

She expects her followers to engage in good deeds to bring warmth and life to the world, and actively defend others from darkness and evils. She also encourages them to embrace their passions of life through love and celebration. Her followers form two major orders, the Order of the Radiant Dawn and the House of Golden Light. The House of Golden Light is the major organization of Soleilites and is made up of mostly scholars and priests. It is focused on charity. They establish temples that are open to the community as a hub of socialization and leisure. The Order of the Radiant Dawn are the defenders of the faith, and are made up of clerics and paladins. They are tasked with protecting the weak and defending their temples.

Neesha, Goddess of Darkness and Trickery

Also known as the Dark Mistress. She is the goddess of the night and darkness, and is jealous and deceitful. She is portrayed as a tall, thin woman with pale, ghostly skin and impossibly dark hair. She represents the darkness of night, and is known for her reserved attitude, and the web of plots she weaves in secret. She often quarrels with Soleil. Her symbol is a black crescent with a white star.

Neesha expects her followers to shun the light of the sun and relish in the darkness of night and starlight, and lure others into her worship. Most people only make offerings to Neesha before they make difficult night journeys, and her temples in cities perform the bare minimum required to satisfy her, and no more. The largest ceremony in honor of Neesha occurs on the winter solstice, to beg her to allow the day to return to prominence. More serious followers of Neesha are more radical, more secretive, and more sinister.

Vodona, Goddess of the World Below

Also known as the Bountiful Lady, The Green One, and the Lady of the Harvest. Mother of Rhoklo and Dhedros. She oversees the growth and life cycles of all the plants and animals in the world, and is the goddess principally worshipped to ensure a good harvest. She is also a goddess of healing medicine and recipes, and is worshipped by healers. Her symbol is a golden tree with green leaves.

Rhoklo, God of Stone and Earth

Also known as The Builder or The Thinker. He is the god of the stones of the earth, and of building and technology. He is called upon to bless new buildings, and for inspiration by mechanics, artists, and other creatives. His symbol is a gray chisel crossed with a brown paintbrush.

Dhedros, God of the Waves

Also known as The Changing One, or The Violent One. God of the ocean and everything in it, as well as violence, war, unpredictability, and change. Sailors make offerings to him before journeys, and soldiers before wars. His symbol is a blue wave under a red drop.

The Story of Soleil and Neesha

Soleil and Neesha are the two daughters of Corentin, God of the World Above. The two young deities were lively rivals, always trying to outdo each other when playing games. Soleil, who was gifted to be radiantly beautiful and incredibly ambitious, was quick to gain their father’s favor. One day, he gifted her the Sun as a token of his affection. Soleil used it to illuminate the world and filled it with life. She spent her time walking amongst the many plants, animals, and people who relied upon her gifts. Neesha grew quite jealous of her sister, and the Dark Mistress plotted to take the hearts and minds of the beings who so loved Soleil.

She lured mortals to the Underdark and tricked them into agreeing to give Soleil spiked wine during a special festival. Upon tasting the poisoned drink, Soleil fell into an unnatural slumber. While her sister was incapacitated, Neesha broke off a piece of the Sun to take as her own and pushed the Sun from the sky. The fracture spilled millions of tiny fragments of light into the now darkened heavens to form the stars. She then rounded the stolen sunshard to form the Moon. She showed her nightscape creation to their father, who was enamored by the beautiful sight. He declared that the two twins will share the world, taking equal time to provide day and night.

Feeling betrayed by her sister, Soleil swore she would do everything in her power to take back the light that was stolen from her. It is said that every morning the Brilliant Lady’s scorching light drives away her treacherous sister’s darkness in hopes of reclaiming what was once hers. ❦
