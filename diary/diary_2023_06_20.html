Tuesday, June 20, 2023

This is my first diary entry on this site. I plan on using the diary section as a disorganized info-dump of notes and ideas, which aren’t polished enough to count as blog posts. The notes will mostly be about my research, books I’m reading, what I plan on doing in the future, and so on. So, here are some of the things I plan on doing.

(Re)-Learning Math and Physics

Mathematics is the language in which the universe is written and, as an inhabitant of the universe, I feel like I have a quasi-spiritual obligation to understand it before I die. I did study math and physics in college, but not with the rigor that I now wish I had. It’s difficult to justify spending time on it, though, especially with other things to do.

My plan, whenever I get around to it, is to read the following textbooks for a broad overview of pure mathematics and basic physics, taking my time and doing most of the exercises, before deciding what to read next. I have not read any of the math textbooks before, but some of the physics books (Goldstein, Jackson, and Reif) are the ones I used in college. I am indebted to John Baez's How to Learn Math and Physics for choosing the others.

Mathematics

  1. Cheng, The Joy of Abstraction
  2. Lawvere & Schanuel, Conceptual Mathematics
  3. Aluffi, Algebra: Chapter 0
  4. Munkres, Topology
  5. Rudin, Principles of Mathematical Analysis

Physics

  1. Feynman, Lectures on Physics
  2. Goldstein, Classical Mechanics
  3. Jackson, Classical Electrodynamics
  4. Cohen-Tannoudji, Quantum Mechanics
  5. Reif, Fundamentals of Statistical and Thermal Physics
  6. Taylor & Wheeler, Spacetime Physics
